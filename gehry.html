ABOUT

"Architecture should speak of its time and place, but yearn for timelessness."

Frank Gehry, a Canadian-born American architect and designer, is a Pritzker Prize-winning master architect known for his iconic Deconstructionist architectural style. His work is defined by the use of curved surfaces that appear to flutter and the innovative use of materials such as corrugated steel and chain-link fencing. Gehry's early life and influences in Los Angeles, along with his work at Victor Gruen Associates, shaped his unique approach, often described as 'Architect Like An Artist.' His unorthodox use of everyday materials, inspired by his time spent at his grandfather's hardware store, initially faced criticism but later gained recognition. In the 1990s, Gehry established a technology research center and introduced the 'CATIA' system for shaping metal panels to realize his distinctive designs.

SKETCHES

Frank Gehry's sketches exhibit a graffiti-like quality that adds to the uniqueness of his work. These abstract and energetic sketches, characterized by their ambiguity, are a powerful source of inspiration for Gehry, his colleagues, and clients. They continually inform the design and creative process of his buildings.
